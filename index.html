Why today’s youth need to learn how to code.

Improved Analytical & Critical Thinking Skills

97.1% of teachers agree that coding develops critical thinking skills among students.

Future-proof your kids

Experienced computer programmers are in demand and with the advancement of technology, there are increasing career opportunities arising every day. Employees who can code are the future and are highly sought after in any industry.

Coding teaches increasingly in demand skills.

"By learning to code, you can build things that can change the world. And that's an exciting opportunity that I hope more people will take advantage of." - Bill Gates

Many studies indicate that computer programming supports high-level abilities for everyone such as creative thinking, questioning, problem solving and critical thinking which are considered within the scope of 21st-century abilities.

Why we created CODERA

Other platforms didn’t live up.

After searching everywhere online: scouring through books, youtube videos, and coding platforms, we realized that there had to be a better way to effectively teach kids how to code.

The Problems with current platforms

Kids lose interest and become unmotivated.

Initially, we were surprised to find that most kids thought learning to code was boring. We soon realized that the issue wasn’t with the kids, but rather with the resources they were using.

With the right tool, kids become enthusiastic and passionate about coding.

Limited understanding / too complex.

Online coding platforms for kids offer a shallow understanding of programming and don’t develop problem solving abilities, while traditional programming platforms are too complex for kids to learn and enjoy.

Lack help and collaboration.

Most online coding platforms don’t offer assisted instruction and collaboration, making it difficult for kids to get help and learn with peers.

Minimal parent involvement

Parents lack knowledge of the progress their child is making, everything from concepts the child has learned learned to the amount of hours they spent.
